# APA References

This section provides a list of key academic papers, books, and articles that form the foundation of the topics discussed in this textbook. The entries are formatted according to the American Psychological Association (APA) 7th edition style.

## Foundational Robotics

*   Craig, J. J. (2005). *Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control* (3rd ed.). Pearson Prentice Hall.

*   Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016). *Springer Handbook of Robotics*. Springer.

*   Spong, M. W., Hutchinson, S., & Vidyasagar, M. (2020). *Robot Modeling and Control* (2nd ed.). Wiley.

## Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

*   Goodfellow, I., Bengio, Y., & Courville, A. (2016). *Deep Learning*. MIT Press.

*   Russell, S. J., & Norvig, P. (2020). *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.). Pearson.

## Reinforcement Learning

*   Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018). *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (2nd ed.). MIT Press.

*   Mnih, V., Kavukcuoglu, K., Silver, D., Rusu, A. A., Veness, J., Bellemare, M. G., ... & Hassabis, D. (2015). Human-level control through deep reinforcement learning. *Nature*, *518*(7540), 529-533.

*   Schulman, J., Wolski, F., Dhariwal, P., Radford, A., & Klimov, O. (2017). Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1707.06347*.

## Embodied Cognition & Humanoid Robotics

*   Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. C. (2006). *How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence*. MIT Press.

*   Kajita, S., Hirukawa, H., Harada, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirano, H. (2014). Introduction to Humanoid Robotics. *Springer*.

*   Brooks, R. A. (1991). Intelligence without representation. *Artificial Intelligence*, *47*(1-3), 139-159.
